# Journal

Wout Burgers

# Week 1

In the first week, the main focus was to form a group and to get the idea on what the system should do. I received an email from some classmates who were looking for one more team members, so I decided to join their group. When I joined the group, they had already an idea on what the system could look like. Which would be a smart home system that suggests how the user should automate their home by learning their routines. It sounded interesting to me and I agreed to the system. After that we setup the gitlab and the post was made.

# Week 2

In the second week, we decided to have a meeting on campus to get to know each other better and to discuss further on the system. We discussed for a few fours several aspects of the system and made a presentation about our plan. We looked at the current problems of other systems and how our system could improve this. We looked at the solution direction and what our system should do at least. We started thinking about the proof of concept but personally I had a hard time really finding a suitable aspect for the proof of concept. Finally we made a logistics plan to organize the workflow and to set a weekly meeting with the team.

# Week 3

We started writing on our report for the Finch release. We got together at the start of the week to divide the plan and to make a deadline within the group to finish the parts of the report. I got to write on the stakeholder analysis. I first brainstormed on who the stakeholders are. I wrote them down on a piece of paper and I did some more research on the system and who it would affect. When I was satisfied with the list I started writing them out, going from the most obvious and important stakeholders to the least. It took quite a while to write it all out and to thoroughly explain why they have their interest in this way. It was also some while ago that I had to write such a text so I spent around 8 hours on this part. Later that week we had a discussion with the group as the requirements of the report were not fully clear. And after some discussion I put everyone's part together to finalise our report for the Finch release.

# Week 4

On Monday, we started by making the slides for the presentation with the other group. We spent about 2 hours creating the presentation. In the beginning, we were not really sure if what we were doing was okay, but when receiving positive feedback from the TA I felt relieved. That evening we also pushed the report to gitlab. After the meeting with the other group and the TA we had a meeting to discuss our current state of the report and see what needed improvement. I forgot to visualise the stakeholders so I made the diagram and integrated it with the report which took about an hour. Later that week we started further brainstorming on ideas for the poc but we all still felt a bit stuck.

# Week 5

We decided to have a meeting on campus to work and further discuss on our poc plan. We all wrote down some ideas on the poc before the meeting and we discussed for roughly an hour on what our direction would be. I currently had the most interest in looking at the availability part of the system which in the end we also agreed on to go for as it was the most suitable for our poc. I also felt that doing this aspect of the system was the best to be able to create a strong poc. We discussed on how we would setup this small project. We also thought about what architectural decisions would we have to make and how we would evaluate it. I was still not 100% sure so we decided to post a chat in the mattermost to ask the TA. After this we agreed on the idea.

# Week 6

The week started somewhat slow which frustrated me a bit, it took quite some time to get a response from the group. Eventually we managed to plan a meeting, during this meeting we went over the poc and divided some more work. I got the task to work on the completion of the problem analysis and to find which open source components could be used for our system. I dived into which parts of the system could be implemented with open source components (2 hours) and after this I went to research for each part which open source components could be used (3 hours). I wrote all down findings in the report (3 hours). Next, I started working on the problem analysis, I transformed the goal and the problem statement into an introduction (1 hour) and I rewrote the stakeholder analysis to better align with the diagram and explain why they have certain interest and influence (1.5 hours). I hope to finish the report as soon as possible but I feel like that some group members don't have the same intentions.

# Week 7

As a feedback, we got that we did not use the gitlab reviews as much as we should, so I started off the week reviewing some of the work of the other group members. Which I spent roughly an hour on. We finished up our report and handed it in which I spent some more hours on. During the week I reviewed a different report which I spent about 3 hours on. It was a bit more than I expected. After the review of our own report, I started looking into creating a pipeline for the poc but did not get the chance yet to implement it, which is the plan for next week. To finish off the week I took some time to go over the feedback we received.
